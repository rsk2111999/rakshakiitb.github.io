  • Aerodynamics

  • The Aerodynamic subsystem is responsible for the design and optimisation of the aircraft. This subsystem captures and models in accordance with the user requirements, design, analyse and optimize the aircraft. This subsystem is categorised as such:
    • Conceptual Sizing - Study the user requirements and accordingly design the nascent framework of the plane.
    • Flight Dynamics - Study the performance, stability and controls of the aircraft and accordingly change the design parameters to best suit the desired objectives.
    • Analysis - Simulate and analyse the aircraft in virtual settings via dedicated softwares and optimize the design while collating with live test results.

  • Structures

  • Structures is responsible for
    • CAD design - Modelling each component of the plane and assembling them, to help in manufacturing, structural and aerodynamic analysis, final assembly.
    • Stress analysis - Each component is structurally analyzed to ensure none of them break or deform out of bounds due to the stresses in flight.
    • Material Selection - Appropriate materials are selected to ensure components have enough strength to withstand the loads while being as light as possible
    • Wing load testing - Wings undergo ground testing to ensure they do not break under the large loads they are subjected to in flight

  • Software

  • The Software subsystem is responsible for the onboard processing of the images collected during the mission. This includes identifying the potential targets, classifying them and geotagging them for future purpose.
    The subsystem is categorized into:
    • Image Processing and Machine Learning - Identifying the potential targets and classifying them with precision
    • Parallel Programming - Responsible for faster implementation of the image processing algorithms
    • Interoperability - Responsible for autonomous update of detected targets to the server

  • Controls and Communication

  • The subsystem conducts regular test for sensors calibration and adjusting the gains of the controller. The UAV is given various flight paths during the test assess the performance of the UAV and according modify the control parameters to achieve acceptable performance. Communication is responsible for proper communication between plane and ground control station It is also responsible for interoperability i.e, autonomous upload of collected data to remote server.
    The aim of this subsystem is to achieve the following for an UAV:
    • Autonomous Navigation
    • Autonomous Takeoff
    • Autonomous Landing
    • Obstacle Avoidance
    • Uninterrupted Communication

  • Propulsion

  • The Propulsion subsystem involves in the selection of a suitable power plant for the UAS(Unmanned Aerial System) in accordance with the requirements of the other major subsystems. The subsystem focuses on optimal selection of propellers for the chosen power plant maximizing its efficiency. Suitable selection of batteries and maintaining a full catalogue of equipments also falls under this subsystem.
